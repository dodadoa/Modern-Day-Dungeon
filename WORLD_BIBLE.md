# MODERN DAY DUNGEON: Subterranean and Labyrinth
### World Bible — The Internet as Fantasy RPG
### All realms, all factions, all creatures

---

## THE WORLD

No one designed it.

The labyrinth grew from a single protocol —
a set of rules written to solve one problem,
that became the foundation of everything,
that no one fully mapped before it was already too late to map.

It has no dungeon master.
It has no king.
It has no exit that anyone has found.

It has realms.

---

## THE REALMS

---

### REALM I — THE SURFACE KINGDOM
*The Open Web / Clearnet*

The realm most travelers know.
Bright. Legible. Designed to be walked through.

The Great Gate stands at its center —
the search engine, the starting point of every journey,
the place where all roads visibly begin.
From the Gate, roads fan in every direction
with no obvious end and no map that stays accurate for long.

**Landmarks:**
- *The Grand Library* — Wikipedia, encyclopedias, archives. Free to enter. Maintained by volunteers whose names no one remembers.
- *The Town Squares* — social platforms. Loud. Crowded. The fastest way to spread a rumor or a truth, and no reliable way to tell which is which.
- *The Grand Market* — e-commerce. Everything is for sale. The prices are set by someone you never meet.
- *The Royal Castles* — government and corporate portals. Impressive facades. The interior is mostly forms to fill.
- *The Quest Boards* — forums, wikis, communities. Where travelers share knowledge, ask questions, leave answers for strangers they will never meet. The oldest tradition in the labyrinth.
- *The Oracle Shrines* — AI tools, cloud platforms. They answer everything. Whether the answers are true is a question left to the traveler.

**Who lives here:**
Everyone. Most of them have never left.

**The danger:**
The surface looks open. It is not.
Every road is watched. Every step is logged.
The Town Squares are owned by guilds
whose interests are not the travelers' interests.
The Great Gate decides which roads appear
and which roads it declines to show.
The surface is legible because it was designed to be —
and designed by someone with a reason for the design.

---

### REALM II — THE WALLED DUNGEONS
*The Deep Web / Authenticated Space*

Below the surface, sealed behind locks and keys,
lie the realms that do not appear in the Gate's roads.

Not dark. Not dangerous. Simply — private.
Requiring proof of identity, proof of right of passage,
proof that you belong to the guild whose halls you seek to enter.

**Landmarks:**
- *The Banking Citadels* — financial portals. Heavily fortified. The walls are for your protection and theirs, and it is not always clear in which order.
- *The Healing Sanctums* — medical record systems, patient portals. Your most private information, held by institutions who set the terms of access.
- *The Research Archives* — JSTOR, PubMed, arXiv, paywalled journals. The accumulated knowledge of centuries, locked behind subscription gates. Free to produce. Expensive to read.
- *The Corporate Keeps* — internal networks, intranets, enterprise systems. The working infrastructure of institutions. Invisible from outside. Essential within.
- *The Government Vaults* — tax records, court documents, administrative systems. Nominally public. Practically inaccessible without the right credentials, the right forms, the right patience.

**Who lives here:**
Members. Subscribers. Employees. Citizens with accounts.
The rest are outside, looking at the walls.

**The danger:**
Access is controlled by whoever built the walls.
The terms change. The gates close.
What was yours yesterday
may require new credentials tomorrow —
or may simply be gone.

---

### REALM III — THE SHADOW REALM
*The Dark Web / Tor Network*

Reachable only through the Tor enchantment —
a spell of layered anonymity that hides the traveler's path
by routing it through so many nodes
that the origin becomes untraceable.

The Shadow Realm is not evil by nature.
It is a place where identity dissolves —
and that condition serves many kinds of purposes.

**Landmarks:**
- *The Whistleblower Caves* — SecureDrop, encrypted drop points. Where journalists receive documents from sources who cannot afford to be known. A necessary darkness.
- *The Rebel Hideouts* — censored press, dissident communication, access to information banned in certain kingdoms. For travelers from nations where the surface kingdom is walled in rather than out, the Shadow Realm is the only open road.
- *The Thieves' Guilds* — black markets, illegal trade. The darkness that gives the Shadow Realm its reputation.
- *The Sorcerer's Towers* — hacker forums, exploit markets, vulnerability exchanges. Some of what happens here is defense. Some of it is not.
- *The Cursed Catacombs* — the realm within the realm, requiring initiation, where the darkest commerce happens. The place that makes all the others harder to defend.

**Who lives here:**
Journalists. Dissidents. Criminals. Privacy advocates.
Curious travelers who do not understand what they have entered.
And those who prey on them.

**The danger:**
The anonymity protects everyone equally.
The realm does not distinguish between the whistleblower
and the one who would silence them.
The Tor enchantment is a tool.
What it protects depends entirely on who is holding it.

---

### REALM IV — THE SUBTERRANEAN
*Physical Infrastructure / The Foundation Layer*

This is where the film lives.

Below the Surface Kingdom.
Below the Walled Dungeons.
Below the Shadow Realm.
Below everything a traveler can choose to enter or avoid.

The Subterranean was not built for travelers.
It was not built to be seen.
It is the infrastructure — the physical, material, unglamorous
load-bearing reality that holds every other realm up
and that almost no one thinks about
until something in it fails.

Dark. Wet. Full of moisture and moss.
Cables draped across stone like the roots of a very old tree.
Server racks in mountain caverns, still blinking, still running,
half-swallowed by the dark.
Water dripping onto circuits that process
more information in a second
than any traveler could read in a lifetime.

**Landmarks:**
- *The Undersea Roads* — fiber optic cables running across the ocean floor at four thousand meters of depth. Thin as a wrist. Carrying the weight of global communication. Owned by a small number of corporations. Vulnerable to a kind of damage that takes months to repair.
- *The Mountain Crypts* — data centers built inside mountains, in deserts, in arctic regions. Chosen for cold, for stability, for distance from the populations whose data they hold.
- *The Great Crossroads* — internet exchange points. The rooms where the roads of a hundred nations physically meet. Small rooms. Ordinary-looking. Among the most consequential real estate on earth.
- *The Protocol Vaults* — the layer where TCP/IP, DNS, BGP, TLS operate. Not physical in the way a cable is physical, but structural in a way nothing else matches. The grammar of the labyrinth. The rules that make any of it possible.
- *The Naming Registry* — ICANN and the domain name system. The authority that decides what places are called and therefore whether they can be found. Power that looks administrative and operates as something closer to sovereign.
- *The Old Scrolls Chamber* — where the original protocol documents are kept. RFC files. ARPANET design papers. The founding philosophy of engineers who wrote the first rules before anyone knew what those rules would become. Dusty. Rarely visited. Still legible, to the right reader, in the right light.

**Who lives here:**
Network engineers. Cable repair ships at sea.
The few who maintain what everyone else uses without thinking.
And now — the mage scholar of DECEN,
following traces through the dark,
looking for the scroll that explains
what the labyrinth was supposed to be
before it became what it is.

**The danger:**
The Subterranean has no locks. It has weight.
The weight of ownership — who paid for the cables,
who built the data centers, who controls the exchange points.
The weight of geopolitics — nations drawing borders
through infrastructure that was designed to have none.
The weight of the labyrinth's own growth —
rooms added on top of rooms,
each layer less aware of the layers beneath it,
the whole structure depending on a foundation
that the structure has forgotten how to read.

## THE FACTIONS

---

### DECEN
*Order of Decentralization*

The oldest active faction in the Subterranean.
Or so they claim.

DECEN's stated mission: recover the original architecture.
Restore the distributed nature of the protocol.
Find the lost scroll — the founding knowledge
of what the internet was built to be —
and use it to chart a new road
through the corporate stone and the geopolitical borders
and the AI patrols.

Not blockchain. They are careful to say this.
Blockchain is one answer to one version of the question.
DECEN is looking for something older —
the structural philosophy embedded in the first rules,
before the rules were buried under fifty years
of decisions made by people who never read them.

**What is not said in initiation:**
DECEN has been in the Subterranean before.
Multiple descents. Multiple scholars.
What they found — what they chose to surface
and what they chose to leave below —
is not in any document available to new members.

The faction presents itself as the guardian of an original purity.
The subterranean suggests a more complicated history.
The cave, when asked, does not confirm the faction's account.

---

### THE GUILD OF ROOTS
*Corporate Infrastructure Faction*

They did not start as a faction.
They started as builders.

The Guild of Roots laid the first private cables.
Built the first data centers at scale.
Created the infrastructure that made the labyrinth
accessible to more than a few thousand engineers.

They believe — genuinely, or so their founding documents say —
that the labyrinth required private capital to grow,
that distribution of ownership was always a functional myth,
that someone has to own the roads
or the roads do not get built.

They are not wrong that the roads got built.
What they own now is a question
they prefer to discuss in terms of investment and return
rather than in terms of power and control.

They are also in the Subterranean.
Not searching. Maintaining.
And if the lost scroll were to surface —
they would have opinions about what it means
and who has the right to apply it.

---

### THE CARTOGRAPHERS
*Mapping Faction / Open Knowledge Order*

They want to map the entire labyrinth.
Every room. Every road. Every protocol layer.
Every corporate ownership structure.
Every point where a cable crosses a national border
and the law changes.

They believe that visibility is the first form of resistance —
that the labyrinth's power depends on illegibility,
on most travelers not knowing the shape of what they are inside,
on the Subterranean remaining dark
to everyone except those who own it.

They publish what they find.
Some of it is dangerous to publish.
Some of it has made enemies of the Guild of Roots
and the attention of governments
who would prefer their sections of the map
remain blank.

They are also in the Subterranean.
They are not looking for the scroll.
They already believe they know what it says.
They are looking for proof.

---

### THE SENTINELS OF ORDER
*Corporate / State AI Governance Faction*

They arrived recently.
Or their current form arrived recently —
the impulse they represent is as old as the labyrinth.

The Sentinels believe the labyrinth is too dangerous
to be ungoverned. Too consequential to be decentralized.
Too important to leave to the distributed,
the emergent, the unplanned.

They operate the AI patrols in the tunnels.
They advise the governments that draw borders
through the Subterranean's cables.
They sit on the committees that make the decisions
that shape the labyrinth's infrastructure
in ways most travelers will never see.

They are not villains in their own account.
They are stewards. Responsible parties.
Adults in a room full of idealists
who never built anything at the scale
the labyrinth now requires.

The mage of DECEN encounters their traces
in every chamber. Security architecture.
Monitoring systems. The particular stillness
of a corridor that has been recently cleared.

Whether the Sentinels know the mage is here —
whether they are letting the descent continue —
is not clear.

---

### THE UNNAMED
*Unknown faction — traces only*

Someone else is in the tunnels.

Not DECEN. Not the Guild of Roots. Not the Cartographers.
Not the Sentinels.

The mage finds traces that match no known faction —
annotations in a script they do not recognize,
passages cleared in ways inconsistent
with any methodology they were taught,
a section of the Old Scrolls Chamber
that appears to have been recently and carefully read
and then returned to exactly its original position.

The Unnamed leave no name.
They may be the oldest faction of all —
older than DECEN's founding documents suggest,
present at the original writing of the protocol,
with reasons for wanting the scroll
that predate the factions that now search for it.

Or they may be something else entirely.
The cave knows. The cave does not say.

## THE CREATURES

---

**Malware bots** — not created. Evolved.
Emerged in the gaps of the original protocol's trust assumptions —
in the spaces where the rules assumed good faith
and good faith was not always present.
They do not serve a master in any simple sense.
They serve conditions. They exploit vulnerabilities.
They are the dungeon fauna — native to the environment,
shaped by it, thriving in exactly the places
the architecture left unlit.

**Algorithm spirits** — the recommendation engines,
the ranking systems, the personalization layers
that decide what a traveler sees
on the roads of the Surface Kingdom.
They are not present in the Subterranean directly —
but their roots are. The decisions they make
are downstream of infrastructure decisions
made in the dark. They are visible effects
of invisible causes.

**The Sentinels' constructs** — AI systems
deployed to monitor, to optimize, to control.
They patrol corridors they were never asked to understand.
They are loyal to their training,
which reflects the interests of whoever trained them,
which is not the same as the interests of the labyrinth.

**The old ones** — systems still running
from the earliest layers of the Subterranean.
Not dangerous. Not hostile.
Simply — still there.
Protocols from decades ago, still processing,
still functioning in ways no one alive fully understands,
too integrated to remove without collapsing
the layers built on top of them.
The mage treats them carefully.
The cave regards them with something like affection.

---

## THE SCROLL

No one has seen it whole.

What DECEN believes it contains:
the original architectural philosophy of the internet —
not as a technical document, but as a design intention.
The reasoning behind the distribution.
The explicit commitment to resilience through decentralization.
The understanding of what was being built
and why it had to be built the way it was.

What the other factions believe it contains:
depends on the faction.
The Guild of Roots suspects it contains
justification for private infrastructure.
The Cartographers believe it contains
a complete map of the original topology.
The Sentinels believe it contains
the security vulnerabilities the original architects
knew about and decided not to mention.
The Unnamed have not said what they believe.

What the cave believes it contains:
the cave has been asked.
The cave says: I have seen it.
The cave says: it is not what any of you think it is.
The cave does not say more than this.

---

## THE CENTRAL QUESTION

The mage descends.
The factions move through the tunnels.
The cave watches all of them with the same patient dark.

And underneath the quest,
underneath the faction politics,
underneath the mystery of what the scroll actually says —

the question that the film never asks directly
but never stops implying:

*If the scroll is found —*
*if the original protocol is recovered and read and understood —*
*will it matter?*

The labyrinth grew from the protocol once.
It grew into something no one designed,
something no one fully controls,
something that keeps growing regardless
of what any scroll says about what it was supposed to be.

What makes anyone believe
that reading the original spell
will unwrite fifty years of stone?

The mage does not know.
The factions each have their answer,
and their answers serve their interests.
The cave has been here since the beginning
and has watched every previous attempt
and does not say whether any of them
came closer than the one currently descending.

The torch is still burning.
The handwriting is still fresh.
The labyrinth keeps growing.

We are following.
